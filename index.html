About Me


Hello, I am Emma (they/them), I am a Melbourne based digital designer.

I love making and learning new things, especially if they are a bit weird, squids my current interest. It has led to me developing a wide range of skills and interests. However, interactive design specifically websites, games or installations are the areas I keep coming back to. I want me designs to connect individuals to the world around them creating connections and hope for the future, especially when it comes to environmental issues.

Works

Newport Lakes Interactive Map


This website began as a university project and became something I have continued working on. It is based on a local nature reserve, Newport Lakes, detailing its history, my connection to it and the impact is has on the community and ecology. It is designed to get more people involved in its conservation groups, helping to improve and protect it for the future.

It is an interactive map with ten locations of significance marked. Each marker has a popup with an image and a field recording taken form the location. Clicking on the image will take the user to more images and a voice over and transcript detailing information about the location. Each covering a different theme ranging from history, community building or ecological protection and issues.

The site is meant to be used at the park to allows people to learn more about it, building visitors connection to the park and their subsequent desire to get involved and protect the reserve. So, it is designed primarily for use on a phone, though limited desktop optimisation had been done. I am aware that people will be accessing the site from outside the park, so it field recordings and imagers have been included to get a sense of the space and changing locations, serving as a case study for successful community lead conservation efforts, and encouraging uses to find similar group in their area.

While starting as a university assignment the website has become much more to me. I have contacted local groups like the council to see if they want to help develop or promote the website further.
